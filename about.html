About

A career built inside businesses, not around them.

I have spent more than two decades working in technology businesses across Europe, the Middle East, India and Africa.

I have never been attracted to easy businesses.

What has always interested me is the moment when growth slows, decisions become harder, and the business needs more than another plan.

In those moments, the real work is usually not about adding more ideas. It is about understanding what is actually happening, aligning people around what matters, and rebuilding the discipline to move.

My experience comes from operating inside businesses. Leading commercial organizations. Opening markets. Building teams. Working with customers and partners. Restoring momentum when performance becomes unclear.

I do not believe leadership is about having all the answers. I believe it is about creating the conditions where the right answers can appear, be challenged, and become action.

What keeps me interested

Complexity reveals the real quality of leadership.

When things are going well, many weaknesses stay hidden. When pressure arrives, everything becomes visible: the quality of decisions, the strength of trust, the clarity of priorities and the discipline of execution.

I do my best work where the easy answers have already failed.

How I think

Four beliefs that guide my work.

01

Reality comes first.

Before changing direction, leaders need the courage to see the business as it really is.

02

Execution is a culture.

It is built through rhythm, accountability and people who understand what matters.

03

Markets are human.

Expansion is not only geography. It is trust, timing, culture and local judgment.

04

Momentum must be protected.

Growth disappears slowly when difficult decisions are avoided for too long.

If this way of thinking resonates, let’s continue the conversation.
